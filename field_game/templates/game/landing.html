How well can you protect a farm against infectious disease?

Introduction

In the FIELD project we are interested in how farmers make decisions when it comes to preventing their cows from getting disease. We have built a game to test this decision making process and are asking you to play it.

We will record your decisions as you play the game and use this in our analysis. We will not collect any personal information.

Background

Beef and dairy products go through a complex supply chain before they reach the supermarket shelf. To maintain a steady income, farmers are under pressure to get the most out of their land and their livestock. This often means buying in new younger cows from other farms and selling off the older ones for the right price.

With this movement comes a substantial risk of spreading diseases. Many of the diseases affecting cows are endemic. These diseases are not deadly, but they are unpleasant for cows. They cause difficulties for farmers too because affected cows produce less milk. To combat endemic diseases farmers can test, treat, and vaccinate their animals.

What we want you to do

We want you to play a game based around the struggles of being a cattle farmer. Your goal is to make money for your farm by producing milk and trading cows with other farmers. Some cows are infected with a disease. If they go untreated it will spread to other cows in the herd and stop them from producing milk.

How to play

You will be asked to choose a character. When you enter the game, you will see your character standing at your farm. You will also see 5 other characters standing next to their farms. They are your opponents.

Each player starts with £20 and a pen containing 8 cows. Cows can do three things:

  1. Make money for the farm by producing milk
  2. Be bought and sold to other farms
  3. Carry an infection which prevents them from producing milk

The game proceeds over 6 rounds. Each round consists of two decisions.

Decision 1: to treat or not treat your cows for infection

Tap on the hospital in the middle of the screen to 'treat' your cows. This will cost £5 and it will make any infected cows healthy. Cows returning from the hospital are blue to show that they have been treated. Tap on your farm to choose to 'not treat' your cows. This costs nothing, but any infected cows on the farm will remain infected.

Decision 2: who to buy from

Tap on the pen of another farm to bring one of their cows onto your own farm. You will pay £5 to the owner of the farm you chose.

There is one restriction regarding who you can buy from: if your cows are treated, you can only choose farms that have treated cows. If your cows are untreated, you can only choose farms that have untreated cows.

Tip: When you buy from another farm there is a risk that it might be infected. Keep an eye on the infection risk level indicator in the top right of the screen and try to choose farms that you think are most likely to sell you a healthy cow.

At the top right of the screen is an infection risk level indicator. This indicates how many cows have disease in the game.

End of the round
At the end of the round two things happen:

  1. Cows are milked: each farmer receives £1 for every healthy cow on their farm
  2. Infection spreads: infected cows transmit the disease to other cows in the same pen

Winning the game

The game ends after the 6th round.
The winner of the game is the player who has the most money in the bank.

You can review the rules at any time by tapping the ? in the top right corner of the screen.

Play the game

For our experiment we want you to play the game three times. We would like to see how much you improve as you play. No personal data will be collected
